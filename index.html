The Three Phases
Race Fuel → The Cut → Hold & Perform. Macros, rules, and transition triggers.
The architecture is bulletproof. But if the fuel is wrong, the engine doesn't care how good the blueprint is.
The Math
Starting point: 44 years old, 5'10", 218 lbs. Training two-a-days (AM running, PM S&C) 5–6 days per week. Target: 185–190 lbs at below 11% body fat.
BMR (Mifflin-St Jeor): ~1,875 cal/day
TDEE: 1,875 × 1.75 (very active) = ~3,280 cal/day at current weight. Recalculate at each phase transition.
Protein Floor: 200g/day across all three phases. 1g per pound of target lean body mass. Non-negotiable. Protein protects muscle during the deficit, supports recovery between two-a-days, and is the most satiating macronutrient.
Protein stays at 200g in every phase. It's the fixed point around which everything else moves.

Phase 1 — Race Fuel (Feb 24 – Apr 19)
Goal: Fuel the half marathon build and two-a-day training while losing weight at a sustainable rate. Performance is the priority. You are earning the right to cut.
2,900 cal/day (~380 below TDEE). Enough to lose 0.75–1 lb/week (6–8 lbs over 8 weeks) while keeping energy high. Expected race-day weight: 210–212 lbs.
Protein: 200g (800 cal, 28%) — Muscle preservation and recovery between two-a-days.
Carbs: 300g (1,200 cal, 41%) — Jet fuel. Glycogen for AM runs and PM S&C. Carbs are not the enemy during a half marathon build. They are the primary performance driver.
Fat: 100g (900 cal, 31%) — Hormone support, satiety, joint lubrication. Minimum healthy fat for a male athlete is ~0.4g/lb.
Phase 1 Rules
Carbs cluster around training windows. Pre-run, post-run, pre-S&C, post-S&C. Dinner carbs are earned by training, not by the clock.
Protein spread across all 5 feeding windows (35–45g per window). No window drops below 30g.
Long run days (Sat/Sun): Increase carbs by 50g. Pull from fat, not protein. This is a fueling day, not a diet day.
Hydration: Half your body weight in ounces minimum (109 oz/day). More on run days. Electrolytes on runs over 60 minutes.
Processed sugar: gone. Alcohol: gone. These two decisions alone eliminate most of the caloric surplus.
Phase 2 — The Cut (Apr 20 – ~Aug/Sep)
Goal: Drive from 210–212 to 185–190. This is where the weight comes off. Running volume drops to maintenance (3 runs/week, no race build), S&C becomes the primary engine. The deficit opens.
2,500 cal/day (~650 below estimated TDEE at ~210 lbs). This yields 1.25–1.5 lbs/week. 20–25 lbs in 18–22 weeks puts you at target by August–September.
Protein: 200g (800 cal, 32%) — Stays locked. Higher percentage now because total calories dropped. Protein is the firewall against muscle loss in a deficit.
Carbs: 225g (900 cal, 36%) — Reduced from Phase 1 but still sufficient for S&C. Running volume lower, so glycogen demand drops. Carbs still cluster around training.
Fat: 89g (800 cal, 32%) — Slightly reduced. Stays above the 0.4g/lb floor at ~200 lb midpoint.
Phase 2 Rules
Recalculate TDEE every 10 lbs lost. As weight drops, TDEE drops, and the deficit narrows if calories stay the same. Adjust by 100–150 cal per 10-lb milestone.
Carb cycling: Training days get 250g carbs. Rest days drop to 175g. Difference comes from removing pre/post workout carb windows. Protein and fat stay constant.
One refeed day per DECAD: Bring calories to maintenance (full TDEE) with the extra coming from carbs. Resets leptin, refills glycogen, keeps metabolism from adapting down. Not a cheat day — a strategic tool.
If weight loss stalls for 2+ weeks at the same calorie level, add one S&C session before reducing calories further. Movement before restriction.
If energy tanks, mood drops, or session quality degrades for 3+ consecutive days, you're cutting too hard. Add 200 calories from carbs and reassess.
Phase 3 — Hold & Perform (Sep – Dec 31+)
Goal: Land at 185–190, stabilize, and ride the final 100+ days of the encyclopedia at fighting weight. No deficit. No surplus. Maintenance.
~2,800 cal/day (estimated maintenance at 185–190 lbs, same activity level). Recalculate TDEE with actual weight at phase entry.
Protein: 190g (760 cal, 27%) — Slight reduction from 200g because you're no longer in a deficit and muscle preservation pressure drops. Still well above 1g/lb lean mass.
Carbs: 275g (1,100 cal, 39%) — Restored to fuel full-intensity training without the drag of a deficit. Performance ceiling lifts.
Fat: 95g (855 cal, 31%) — Restored to comfortable levels.
Phase 3 Rules
Weigh daily, average weekly. If weekly average drifts more than 3 lbs above target for 2 consecutive weeks, reduce by 150 calories. If it drops more than 3 lbs below, add 150. The thermostat, not the gas pedal.
This is your life. Not a phase. The eating patterns you build here are the ones you keep. December 31 is when the documentation ends. The nutrition doesn't.
The man at the end of this project doesn't go back to how he ate before. He already forgot how.
Phase Transition Triggers
Don't switch phases by the calendar. Switch by the conditions.

Five feeding windows across the day, spaced every 3–4 hours. Built around the two-a-day training rhythm. Protein in every window. Carbs cluster around training.

Window 1 — Post-run. Largest carb window. Glycogen refill after AM miles.
Window 2 — Bridge meal. Keeps blood sugar stable. Prevents afternoon crash.
Window 3 — Largest meal by volume. Pre-loads for PM session (3–4 hours out).
Window 4 — Pre-S&C fuel. Light, fast-digesting. 60–90 min before PM session.
Window 5 — Post-S&C. Lower carb unless long/intense session. Recovery focus.
Adjustments
Fasted AM Run (5:00–5:30 AM)
Have 20–30g fast-digesting carbs before the run (banana, handful of dates, or a slice of toast with honey). This is not a meal. It's ignition fuel. Full breakfast shifts to post-run. Everything else stays the same.
PM-Only Training Days
If no AM run (rest day from running, S&C only in PM): Breakfast carbs drop by ~50g. Mid-morning snack stays. Afternoon snack becomes the pre-workout window. Same total protein. Calories auto-correct because the run-day carb window is removed.
Full Rest Days
Rare but they'll happen. Drop to 3 meals, skip the snacks, reduce carbs to ~175g (Phase 2) or ~225g (Phase 1). Protein stays at 200g. Fat fills the remaining calories. This is not a punishment day. It's a recovery day. Eat to rebuild.

Sunday Prep System
Checklist, grocery list, and substitution logic. 90 minutes. Zero willpower Mon–Sat.
One prep session per week. Sunday afternoon or evening. 90–120 minutes. This is the nutrition equivalent of "before they wake, after they sleep" — you front-load the effort so that Mon–Sat requires zero willpower.
If the containers aren't loaded Sunday night, Monday's nutrition is already compromised. The prep is the practice.
The Sunday Prep Checklist
1
Protein Cook
4 lbs chicken breast (baked 400°F, 25 min) + 2 lbs ground turkey (browned, seasoned). Covers lunch and dinner protein Mon–Fri.
2
Carb Cook
Large pot white rice (4–5 cups dry) + 3–4 sweet potatoes (baked alongside chicken). Rice and sweet potatoes in containers. Microwave-ready.
3
Veg Prep
Wash and chop broccoli, peppers, zucchini, asparagus. Roast 2 sheet pans of mixed veg. Half raw (for stir-fry later in week), half roasted (grab and go).
4
Snack Assembly
Portion trail mix into bags. Slice turkey roll-ups. Pre-make overnight oats ×3. Snacks are the first thing to fall apart without prep.
5
Breakfast Prep
Batch protein pancakes (freeze in stacks of 2). Hard-boil 12 eggs. Pancakes reheat in 90 sec. Eggs are instant protein.
6
Container Load
Assemble 5 lunch containers (protein + carb + veg). Stack in fridge. Grab one on the way out. Decision eliminated.

Substitution Logic
The pattern stays. The tool changes. If the macro profile matches, the swap works.
Chicken breast →
Turkey breast, pork tenderloin, white fish (tilapia, cod), extra-lean ground beef. Protein-for-protein. Keep fat content similar.
White rice →
Sweet potato, quinoa, couscous, whole wheat pasta, Ezekiel bread. Carb-for-carb. Adjust portions — rice is denser than potato by volume.
Broccoli / asparagus →
Any green vegetable: zucchini, green beans, Brussels sprouts, spinach, kale. Volume swap. Calories are negligible.
Eggs →
Egg whites (double volume) + 1 whole egg. Or liquid egg substitute. Cutting fat: swap to whites. Keeping fat: keep whole.
Greek yogurt →
Cottage cheese, Skyr, casein protein pudding. High-protein dairy swap. Keep sugar low.
Peanut butter →
Almond butter, cashew butter, tahini. Fat-for-fat. Calorie density is nearly identical.
Whey protein →
Plant protein (pea/rice blend), casein, egg white protein. Protein-for-protein. Plant blends may need slightly higher dose for leucine.
Salmon →
Mackerel, sardines, trout, or chicken + fish oil supplement. Omega-3 source. If swapping to non-fish, supplement.
No time to cook →
Rotisserie chicken + microwave rice + bagged salad. The emergency meal. Available at every grocery store. 5 min to plate.
Traveling →
Chipotle bowl (double chicken, rice, fajita veg, no sour cream) or any protein + carb + veg plate. Restaurant protocol: protein visible, carb identifiable, veg present. Skip sauces.
